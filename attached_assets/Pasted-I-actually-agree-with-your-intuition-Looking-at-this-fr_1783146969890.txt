I actually agree with your intuition. Looking at this from the perspective of someone who has worked in CRE servicing and due diligence (and who knows what Kontra is trying to become), the page feels like **three different dashboards stacked together**, each trying to solve the same problem.

The biggest issue isn't the design—it's that the **workflow is backwards**.

---

# 1. Uploading the same document twice ❌

This is probably the biggest UX issue.

For example:

* Due Diligence Checklist

  * Rent Roll ✔
  * T-12 ✔

Then later...

* Financial Overview

  * Upload Operating Statement or Rent Roll

Why?

The system already has those documents.

Instead it should automatically become something like:

```
Financial Overview

✔ Rent Roll
Uploaded by Owner
AI Processed

Occupancy: 90%
Monthly Rent: $36,000

------------

✔ T-12

NOI
DSCR
Expenses
Variance
```

No upload button.

Just analysis.

---

Same thing with

```
Upload Deal Documents
```

Why?

The deal documents are literally already uploaded above.

This entire section should become

```
Document Vault

17 Documents

Purchase Agreement
Rent Roll
T12
Inspection
Insurance
Environmental
Title

View All →
```

## My biggest recommendation

If I were building Kontra, I would reduce this page by **30–40%**.

Keep only:

```
Property Header

↓

Deal Health Score

↓

Due Diligence Checklist
(the ONLY upload area)

↓

AI Findings

↓

Financial Summary

↓

Party Status

↓

Outstanding Items

↓

Timeline

↓

Move to Under Review
```

Everything else either disappears or becomes an automatic summary of documents that have already been uploaded.

---

### One more idea that could make Kontra stand out

Instead of thinking in terms of **documents**, think in terms of **questions the lender needs answered**.

For example, after documents are uploaded, the page could transform into:

```
Can we lend on this property?

✅ Purchase price verified

✅ NOI extracted

⚠ Insurance missing

⚠ 3 life safety violations

✅ Environmental complete

⚠ Title has Schedule B exceptions

Overall readiness

73%

Ready for underwriting after 3 issues are resolved.
```

That shifts Kontra from being a document repository to an **AI underwriting workspace**—which is much closer to the product vision you've been describing.
