Given your situation, I would **tell the developer one thing** and **tell the market another thing**.

Most founders make the mistake of building what they want internally and marketing it the same way externally.

---

# What To Tell The Developer

Tell him:

> We are no longer building a lender platform with a marketplace attached.
>
> We are building a commercial real estate operating system where the property is the center of everything.

Everything should revolve around:

```text
Property
 ├── Documents
 ├── Financials
 ├── Inspections
 ├── Compliance
 ├── Service Providers
 ├── AI Analysis
 ├── Watchlist
 └── Loans
```

Not:

```text
Lender Portal
Borrower Portal
Investor Portal
Servicer Portal
```

Those become permissions behind the scenes.

### Immediate Development Priorities

1. Fix routing between public and logged-in experiences.
2. Create property profiles.
3. Create service provider profiles.
4. Build watchlists.
5. Build AI document review.
6. Build document vault.
7. Build inspection tracking.
8. Build compliance tracking.

Do not spend time building tokenization screens right now.

Do not spend time building lender-specific workflows right now.

Those can stay in the database and codebase but should not be the public-facing experience.

---

# What To Tell The Market

Do NOT say:

> AI Loan Servicing Platform

Do NOT say:

> Real Estate Tokenization Platform

Do NOT say:

> Freddie Mac Compliance Software

Nobody wakes up looking for those things.

Instead say:

> Kontra is the operating system for commercial real estate assets.

# The Long-Term Play

What I think many people miss about your idea is this:

The servicing platform is not the business.

The data is the business.

If Kontra eventually contains:

* 50,000 properties
* Millions of documents
* Inspection histories
* Insurance histories
* Compliance histories
* Vendor relationships

then later you can add:

* Lender monitoring
* Loan surveillance
* Participations
* Tokenization

from a position of strength.

So if I were you, I'd tell the developer:

> Stop thinking about portals.
>
> Start thinking about properties.

And I'd tell the market:

> Kontra helps commercial real estate owners and operators manage assets, inspections, documents, compliance, and risk in one place.

That's a much easier story to sell, discover, and grow while you're still building the company.
